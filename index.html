Origin Story

The Road to Greatness

Every legend has a beginning. Most begin on a high school football field. This one began in a Montréal café with a laptop and a very aggressive LinkedIn outreach strategy.

Niagara Falls, ON

Born Near the Falls

Grew up within earshot of one of the natural wonders of the world. Scientists now believe the constant low-frequency rumble of the falls may have permanently altered his neural pathways, giving him preternatural pocket awareness and an inexplicable tolerance for loud noise (critical for Buffalo home games).

McGill University

Bachelor of Commerce

Graduated from McGill with a B.Com. While most future NFL quarterbacks were running drills, Ilan was running discounted cash flow models. His professors noted his "uncanny ability to read defenses," which at the time they assumed was a metaphor for financial statements. It was not.

Montreal, 2012

Real Ventures & The VC Years

Worked at Real Ventures, Montreal's premier venture capital firm. Evaluated hundreds of startups. Learned to spot talent, identify winners under pressure, and make split-second decisions worth millions. In retrospect, this was obviously quarterback training disguised as a finance career.

McGill, 2013

Founded The Founder Project

Created a student-run VC fund investing in student startups. Covered by BetaKit and BrainStation. Wrote columns about venture capital for national publications. Displayed the leadership, vision, and audacity that would later define his scramble game.

Toronto, 2013–Present

StackedSP & Unstacked Startups

Built StackedSP into one of the most respected tech recruiting firms in Canada, helping clients grow into billion-dollar companies. Also launched the Unstacked Startups podcast. His brother Jared joined after a legendary two-year recruiting effort that Bills GM Brandon Beane called "more impressive than any trade negotiation I've ever seen."

February 2026

Signed by the Buffalo Bills

In a move that shocked the sports world, the Bills signed Ilan Saks to a 7-year, $340 million fully guaranteed contract. When asked if Ilan had any prior football experience, GM Beane replied: "He recruited engineers for billion-dollar startups. You think an NFL blitz scares this man?"

Media Day

Press Conference

Excerpts from Ilan's introductory press conference at Highmark Stadium. Click to expand each Q&A.

ESPN — Adam Schefter

Ilan, you have zero years of professional football experience. How do you respond to the skeptics?

▼
Ilan Saks, QB #18

"Adam, I spent a decade convincing software engineers making $400K in San Francisco to take a pay cut and join a Series A startup with twelve employees and a ping pong table. You think a 260-pound defensive end scares me? I've handled harder objections on a Tuesday morning cold call."

NFL Network — Ian Rapoport

Can you walk us through the moment you decided to leave tech recruiting for professional football?

▼
Ilan Saks, QB #18

"So I was on a podcast recording for Unstacked Startups, interviewing a CTO about AI and talent acquisition. And mid-sentence I just thought: 'I could throw a football 68 miles per hour.' It was like a calling. I hung up, drove to Orchard Park, and threw a spiral through the uprights from the parking lot. The rest, as they say, is history."

The Athletic — Joe Buscaglia

Your brother Jared reportedly took two years to recruit to StackedSP. Will you be recruiting him as your backup QB?

▼
Ilan Saks, QB #18

"Jared's a natural athlete, absolutely. But two years to close him on a business partnership? I'm not waiting two years for a backup quarterback. I told him: training camp starts Monday. He said he needs to think about it. So yeah, we're on year one of what I expect to be a two-year recruitment cycle."

TSN — Darren Dreger

You're Canadian. There are rumors you considered the CFL first. Any truth to that?

▼
Ilan Saks, QB #18

"Darren, I love Canada. I grew up in Niagara Falls, went to McGill, built my company in Toronto. But the CFL field is 110 yards and 65 yards wide. That's too much real estate. In recruiting, we say focus your search. I don't need a wider field. I need a deeper roster. Buffalo was the obvious choice: close to home, passionate fanbase, and tables that need to be jumped through."

Buffalo News — Jay Skurski

How do you plan to connect with the Bills Mafia?

▼
Ilan Saks, QB #18

"I've already started. I went to a tailgate last week, body-slammed a folding table, and then helped three fans update their LinkedIn profiles. One of them got a job offer by halftime. That's what I bring to this franchise: touchdowns AND career development. Bills Mafia isn't just a fanbase. It's a talent pipeline, and I intend to optimize it."
